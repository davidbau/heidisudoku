Heidi's Helpful Hints

This Sudoku workpad is inspired by Heidi's favorite way to solve Sudoku: instead of starting by eliminating candidates one square at a time, she loves to speed along looking for digits that can only be placed in one location because they are blocked by the same digit in various rows, columns, or blocks.

Solving sudoku puzzles by scanning the board for forced single positions is dynamic and fun.

Difficulty Ratings

Good puzzles are rated on a 21-level scale from "Beginner" (the easiest) to "Counfounding" (the hardest).

Puzzles rated "Impossible" are solvable, but the solution may be impossible for a person to find without some lucky guessing.

Puzzles rated "Unsolvable" have no unique solution: an unsolvable puzzle has either no complete solution or multiple different solutions.

Getting Hints

To get a hint, hold down the Hint button. Red squares point out any mistakes. Blue squares will be places that constrain the puzzle in some way, and green squares will be locations where a number can be filled in, or where the possibilities can be narrowed down.

If you are still stuck, you can try a more explicit hint by holding down ctrl while clicking the Hint button. Sometimes the strategies shown will be very subtle: learn to recognize naked pairs and pointing pairs, and see if you can find the hidden pairs or triples or recognize an x-wing or swordfish.

Checking and Fixing Mistakes

The Check button can be used to quickly check if you have penciled in any self-contradictory squares without revealing other hints. Holding ctrl while clicking Check will also reveal harder-to-discover errors by comparing your work to the correct final solution.

When you discover a mistake, you can use the browser's "back" button to undo as many moves as you need to.

Penciling in Candidates

Eventually, you may need to slow down to carefully consider all the candidates at a square. You can click on more than one number to pencil in all the candidates. When you have figured out a number, you can use a double-click to clear the other candidates.

Normally if you choose just one candidate, it will be shown as your answer, but if you really want to just pencil in a little number note, you can click on the icon. Click again to make a that lets you highlight specific numbers.

Candidates everywhere on the board can be automatically eliminated based on your answers so far using the Marks button. To erase all pencilmarks on the entire board, hold down ctrl while clicking Marks.

Loading Puzzles

To enter your own puzzle, hold down ctrl while clicking each square. If you hold down ctrl while clicking Clear, the entire puzzle will be cleared. Notice that the hintpad will not let you enter a puzzle that has no solution.

If you click New Puzzle, a new puzzle with a random level of difficulty will be randomly generated.

Puzzles can also be saved and loaded using the File button.

The Timer

The game will keep track of the amount of time it has taken you to solve a puzzle. Press the Timer button to reveal the elapsed time.

If you walk away from a puzzle and don't want all the idle time counted against you, don't worry: press the "Refresh" button in your browser and the timer will go back to the time you made your last move. Similarly, if you save a game, the time saved will be the time of the last move made.

Credits

Heidi's Hintpad was written in HTML and Javascript by Heidi's husband David Bau using John Resig's jQuery with Ben Alman's handy BBQ plugin to manage "back" button state. Kimberly Geswein's terrific Covered-By-Your-Grace font provides digits that look just like Heidi's handwriting; Jojo Mendoza provided the yellow highlighter icon; and Gordon Royale's mathematically beautiful Minimum Sudoku Collection provides minimal 17-hint puzzles when you hold ctrl while clicking New Puzzle.
