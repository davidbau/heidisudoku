Heidi's Helpful Hints

This electronic Sudoku workpad is inspired by Heidi's favorite way to solve Sudoku: instead of starting by eliminating candidates one square at a time, she loves to speed along looking for digits that can only be placed in one location because they are blocked by the same digit in various rows, columns, or blocks.

Solving sudoku puzzles by scanning the board for forced single positions is dynamic and fun.

Getting Hints

To get a hint, hold down the Hint button. Red squares point out any mistakes. Blue squares will be places that constrain the puzzle in some way, and green squares will be locations where a number can be filled in, or where the possibilities can be narrowed down.

If you are still stuck, you can try a more explicit hint by holding down ctrl while clicking the Hint button. Sometimes the strategies shown will be very subtle: see if you can find the hidden pairs or triples, or if you can recognize an xwing or a swordfish.

Checking and Fixing Mistakes

The Check button can be used to quickly check if you have penciled in any self-contradictory squares without revealing other hints. Holding ctrl while clicking Check will also reveal harder-to-discover errors.

When you discover a mistake, you can use the browser's "back" button to undo as many moves as you need to.

Penciling in Candidates

Eventually, you may need to slow down to carefully consider all the candidates at a square. You can click on more than one number to pencil in all the candidates. When you have figured out a number, you can use a double-click to clear the other candidates.

Candidates can be automatically eliminated based on your answers so far using the Pencilmarks button. To erase all candidates, hold down ctrl while clicking Pencilmarks.

Starting a New Puzzle

To enter your own puzzle, hold down ctrl while clicking each square. If you hold down ctrl while clicking Clear, the entire puzzle will be cleared. The hintpad will not let you enter a puzzle that has no solution.

When you are ready for a new game, click New Puzzle, and a new puzzle will be randomly generated.

Holding ctrl when clicking New Puzzle will load one of Gordon Royale's beautifully minimal 17-hint Sudoku puzzles if you are connected to the internet.
